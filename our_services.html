Our Services

Jump to Eligibility or What You'll Get

The Department for Automation and Remuneration is here to provide you with an income now that you have lost your job to automation. The service is not provided automatically when you lose your job. You must apply on our website and within 3 weeks you will receive a statement which will outline your plan of 12 monthly payments.

You may be asking yourself “who pays for this?”. Since the rise of automation, big corporations, who had previously paid little to no tax, are now required to pay the rightful amount due. With this money, DAR can provide you with an income to help you with your living costs and your reskilling process.Reskilling offers you the chance to re-enter the work sector at a lower risk of being automated.

Eligibility

The Department for Automation and Remuneration requires you to have lost your job due to automation. This means that technology has replaced the process, procedure or job that you used to perform. If you are unsure whether you lost your job to automation you can review your letter of termination as it should specify the reasoning behind your redundancy.

Other factors which will affect your eligibility for receiving the DAR’s universal credit are:

  • You are 18 or over.
  • You are under the State Pension age (or your partner is).
  • You live in the UK.
  • You and your partner have £12,000 or less in savings between you.

Having children does not affect your eligibility for our universal basic income.

If you are 16 or 17 years old:

  • You are not eligible to apply for this universal basic income.
  • You are able to apply for a student grant which is eligible with any digital skill courses.

If you are reskilling or in full time education:

  • You are eligible to apply if you lost your job to automation.
  • You are unable to apply if you are under 18 years old.
  • You are unable to apply if you are on a student visa.
  • You must be a UK citizen.

If you live with your partner:

  • You are eligible to apply.
  • You must be a UK citizen.
  • Your partner’s working status does not affect your due credit.
  • At least one of you must be under the State Pension age.

Eligible Sectors:

  • Retail
  • Engineering and Manufacturing
  • Transport and Logistics
  • Accountancy, Business and Finance
  • Health Care
  • Creative Industries
  • Law
  • Public Services
  • Leisure, Sport and Tourism

You can visit our Sectors page to learn more about these job sectors and how they affect your DAR universal credit plan.

Feel free to use the Contact Us page if you are unsure about your eligibility, unsure which sector category you fall into or feel as if your sector is not covered in our remuneration scheme.

Use the Calculator to get an estimate of the income you could receive monthly.

What You'll Get

At DAR, we want our eligible citizens to receive an income which equates to your skill and time spent at your last job.

The Standard Monthly Allowance is calculated by looking at the average monthly earnings in the given sector.

Standard Monthly Allowance (according to Sector):

Sector Standard Monthly Allowance
Health Care £838.40
Law £1194.40
Engineering and Manufacturing £968
Accountancy, Business and Finance £968
Public Services £854.40
Creative Industries £968
Transport and Logistics £716.40
Leisure, Tourism and Sport £854.40
Retail £544

You can visit the Sectors page to find out what category your previous job falls into. If you feel as if your job sector is not covered by DAR, use our Contact Us page to get in touch. When contacting us make sure to outline your previous job title as well as the roles and skills involved with it.

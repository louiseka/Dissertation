The Process

So, what is the process? Once you have checked your Eligibility you may then start the process of applying for our DAR universal credit which you can do here.

Our team aims to deliver your confirmation and initial universal basic income statement within 3 weeks. You are then either paid on the 1st, 9th or 28th of every month, this depends solely on when you receive your confirmation letter. After a year of being part of our DAR scheme you will have to renew your application with up to date details on your reskilling process and employment status. If you find work whilst you are receiving our DAR universal credit you must Report a Change with us. If you fail to do so you may have to face legal action.

How You're Paid

Each month you are paid your universal credit allowance into your bank, building society or credit union account. If you do not have any of these accounts, we are unable to provide an alternative method of payment, so we advise that you apply for one of them. The payment will be either on the 1st, 9th or 28th of every month.

First Payment:

It will take up to 3 weeks for you to receive a confirmation and statement. Then your first payment will be made on the next available date, 1st, 9th or 28th, and this will be the same date you are paid for the next 11 months. Your confirmation and initial universal basic income statement will state which date your payment is due.

For example:

If you receive your letter on the 31st May, you will receive your payment on the 1st June.

You will then be paid on the 1st of every month after that.

Payment Dates:

If your payment date falls on a bank holiday or the weekend, you will then be paid on the next available working day.

Living with your partner:

The same rules as above apply. The payment will be made to personal accounts of both you and your partner.

How to Claim

Linked here is an online form which you can fill out and submit with the required evidence.

If you live with your partner (regardless if you are not married) you must apply together.

What you need to apply:

  • Your bank, building society or credit union details.
  • An email address.
  • Any details of savings and investments.
  • Proof of redundancy.
  • Proof of identity e.g. driving licence or passport.
  • Proof of home address e.g. a utility letter, bank statement.

Not providing the right information can affect your application and will take longer than 3 weeks. So, if you need help with your application, do not hesitate to Contact Us. The quicker an issue is resolved, the faster you will receive your first payment.
